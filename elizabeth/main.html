Raising Awareness



Walking alone can be very scary and dangerous especially for women and if you feel this way youâ€™re not alone. Many women say that they avoid a nighttime event because they don't want to travel home alone afterward, or an evening jog because of running at night. When doing something simple like going place-to-place, women experience more fear on a larger scale than men, according to the studies. In 27 out of 28 everyday life scenarios, a larger percentage of women indicated more fear. Scott Clement, a polling analyst at The Washington Post, found one data set that begins to speak about how many fear walking alone.



A study from the law firm Farah and Farah showed when walking alone, 58.4% of all women feel much safer when holding keys between their fingers this is a common safety precaution that can be used to defend oneself. Other women said they took precautions like carrying a rock or hammer in their purse, changing a previously walked route, crossing the street, and avoiding eye contact with people. Women also indicate they feel safer when they text or call someone when walking.
