Raising Awareness



Walking alone can be very scary and dangerous especially for women and if you feel this way you’re not alone. Many women say that they avoid a nighttime event because they don't want to travel home alone afterward, or an evening jog because of running at night. When doing something simple like going place-to-place, women experience more fear on a larger scale than men, according to the studies. In 27 out of 28 everyday life scenarios, a larger percentage of women indicated more fear. Scott Clement, a polling analyst at The Washington Post, found one data set that begins to speak about how many fear walking alone.



A study from the law firm Farah and Farah showed when walking alone, 58.4% of all women feel much safer when holding keys between their fingers this is a common safety precaution that can be used to defend oneself. Other women said they took precautions like carrying a rock or hammer in their purse, changing a previously walked route, crossing the street, and avoiding eye contact with people. Women also indicate they feel safer when they text or call someone when walking.



Safety Suggestions

  • Always stay alert and aware of your surroundings, when you are walking anywhere alone.
  • If you can, walk with a friend.
  • Hold your purse, backpack, wallet, any belongings close to your body and in sight
  • Never take your route through back roads, alleys, or cut through wooded areas away from public view
  • If you sense danger or see someone approaching who looks threatening, move quickly to a safe location or find anyone and tell them you don’t feel safe. You can also call 911 immediately, to ensure your protection.
  • Carry a small weapon with you to use in self-defense if needed.
  • Use a panic alert on your cell phone
  • Use an app to track your location
